Visual Identity & Design System Guide

Aditzak — a Basque verb conjugation app. Mascot: the Latxa sheep (mutur beltza variety). Design philosophy: culturally grounded, structurally precise, companion-driven.

Proposed 8 review rounds Mascot: central WCAG-verified
Provenance

How this guide got here

Commissioned from an outside designer as a from-scratch visual identity — no prior brand existed. Went through six review rounds before reaching this state.

  • Round 1 — found a genuine WCAG contrast failure (brand-clay on white text, 3.33:1, below the guide's own 4.5:1 mandate) and a four-way inconsistent dark-neutral scale.
  • Round 2 — fixed both, added the missing lesson-card mockup and a dedicated favicon-scale mark. Its "mathematically verified" contrast ratios turned out not to match independent recomputation — always in the safe direction, but invented rather than calculated.
  • Round 3 — replaced the fabricated decimals with qualitative AA/AAA bands, fixed a table-column mismatch.
  • Round 4 — reconciled a separately-delivered expression-library catalog that had drifted back onto the pre-fix palette and anatomy, and rejected two of its four proposed mascot triggers for conflicting with the anti-guilt voice principle (§ Voice & Tone).
  • Round 5 — audited the guide against the actual React components (not just its own internal consistency) and closed every gap that surfaced: the grammar-badge color system, the hearts-palette question, button/card geometry, motion timings, missing font loading, and icon-system scope.
  • Round 6 — settled the one question round 5 explicitly couldn't: the mascot system is greenlit, and decided to be a central, not incidental, part of the app. See the Mascot Placement Plan below.
  • Round 7 — revisits round 5's "emoji stay" call now that the mascot is central, and proposes a 17-icon system to replace every emoji touchpoint. Documented and asset-complete, deliberately not wired into any component yet.
  • Round 8 — adds worked before/after examples showing what the palette, geometry, and icon decisions look like applied together to three real, currently-shipped components, including what deliberately does not change.
On the contrast numbers in this document Qualitative AA/AAA bands are used throughout rather than decimals. See the Contrast Appendix at the end for independently computed real ratios — use those, not the labels, for anything citable (e.g. a compliance record).

Grammar-accent system (round 5)

The largest block of hardcoded color the component audit found — five arbitrary hues (indigo/rose/blue/purple/amber) with zero mapping in earlier drafts. Rather than invent a fourth unrelated hue set, NOR/NORI/NORK reuse the three brand colors — tying the grammar-role system to the brand instead of adding to the palette.

RoleTokenRationale
NOR (absolutive)brand-forestThe default/core argument every finite verb has.
NORI (dative)brand-txakoliThe "to/for" recipient argument — a secondary accent.
NORK (ergative)brand-clayThe agent argument — paired with the same warm tone used for streaks (also agency-flavored).

Verb type (synthetic/periphrastic) is binary, not a 3-way role, so it moves off color entirely onto a filled-vs-outlined value distinction — which also resolves a real collision: periphrastic verbs and HeartsBadge were both using rose for unrelated meanings.

accent-hearts (standalone exception)

Hearts keep their shipped rose-500/rose-600/rose-100 family, formalized as its own token rather than folded into semantic-warning — a distinct player-economy resource deserves its own identity, the way Duolingo's hearts are red/pink and separate from its green "correct" color. Now that verb type is off rose, it has exactly one meaning in the app.

§6

Motion Principles

Revised in round 5 The original celebration/correction numbers were never checked against the app's actual, already-shipped animations. Documented below is what's real (kept as-is) versus what's genuinely new (the mascot's own reaction, not yet built).

Correction (existing)

animate-shake, 400ms, asymmetric 5-step wobble (−1,2,−4,4,−4px) — already applied app-wide to incorrect answers.

Celebration, per-answer (existing)

animate-flash, 350ms, scale 1 → 1.04 → 1 — already applied to correct answers.

Celebration, mascot-specific (planned, round 6)

Scale 0.98 → 1.03 → 1.0 over 200ms; ears tilt up 10° on correct. New motion, scoped to the mascot avatar only — the per-answer flash above is unaffected.

Navigation (new)

Content slides right-to-left over 300ms on screen change — no existing equivalent (screen changes are currently instant).

The 3-star confetti/firework celebration keeps its own independent 7-color rainbow, deliberately outside the brand palette — a celebration should read as spectacle, not a branded moment. If the mascot appears here, it's alongside the effect, not by recoloring it.

§7

Voice & Tone

Calm, grounded, encouraging — respecting the learner's intelligence and the language's cultural weight. No cartoonish guilt trips.

Acceptable

"Oso ondo! You accurately matched the plural Nork marker."

"Hurbil! Take another look at the dative suffix before trying again."

Avoid

"Wow! Super-duper job! You're a rockstar!"

"You missed your session today. Don't make your sheep cry!"

§8

Accessibility Mandates

  • Dual-Indicator Rule — state changes never rely on color alone; always pair with an explicit mark (✓/✕) and, per the round-6 mascot decision, a corresponding expression change at the placements named in the Mascot Placement Plan, as a third reinforcing signal.
  • Contrast Index — every actionable component meets 4.5:1 minimum.
  • Touch Targets — minimum 48×48px interactive hit zone.

System rules

  • 24×24 viewBox, 2px stroke (2.5px for check/cross), rounded caps and joins throughout.
  • Stroke-based outlines, not filled glyphs — a deliberately different register from the mascot's solid-fill illustration style.
  • stroke="currentColor" in every file — color is set by whatever embeds the icon, not baked into the asset.
Color scoping decision Icons pick up whatever Tailwind color is already present at each usage site (e.g. the streak icon uses the same orange the streak pill already does) — not the brand tokens from §2. The full brand-palette migration (Track A) is still separate and unimplemented; wiring icons to brand hex values now would create visible mismatches until Track A actually ships. Full emoji→icon→color→component mapping is in docs/VISUAL_IDENTITY.md §11.
